Conceptualizing the Chinese American diasporic experience with the metaphor concentric circles, I curated a selection of experiences important to my understanding of identity. All of those within a diaspora are connected to a central axis, a homeland. However, our distances can vary physically, generationally, and culturally. Our identities are complexly layered intersecting with race, class, gender, and sexuality. This is a mere attempt at peeling back my own layers. We all have our own concentric circles.
